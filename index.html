Impressionism ——A art movement

ART TERM

Impressionism is a 19th-century art movement characterized by relatively small, thin, yet visible brush strokes, open composition, emphasis on accurate depiction of light in its changing qualities (often accentuating the effects of the passage of time), ordinary subject matter, unusual visual angles, and inclusion of movement as a crucial element of human perception and experience.

Art and Modernity

Branches

Branches Leaders Introductions
Tradition Renoir, Monet Impressionism coalesced in the 1860s when a group of painters including Claude Monet, Alfred Sisley and Pierre-Auguste Renoir pursued plein air painting together.
Pointillism Georges Seurat An offshoot of Impressionism, Pointillism, otherwise known as Neo-Impressionism, was born in 1886 when Georges Seurat displayed his Sunday Afternoon On The Island of La Grande Jatte and declared the original movement out of date.
Post Vincent van Gogh Post-Impressionists chose to portray not just what was tangible, taking a more symbolic and emotive approach to their subject matter, especially in color use, which was not required to express realism.
